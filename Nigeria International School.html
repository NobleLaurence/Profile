My experience in Nigeria International School

I have been at the very esteemed Nigeria International School (N.I.S.), Embassy of Nigeria, Cotonou, Republic of Benin since January, 1992. In N.I.S., I have been Class Teacher (1992-1995), Assistant Head Teacher (1995-2004), Head Teacher (2004-2015) and Head of Department of Arts and Social Sciences (2017, 2020 till date). I have a wealth of experience in School Administration having played vital roles in the administration of the N.I.S. from 1995 to 2015 during which I ran the Primary School as Head Teacher and participated in the meetings and activities of the School’s Board of Governors chaired by the respective Ambassadors of Nigeria to the Republic of Benin for eleven consecutive years (2004-2015). I have been to the International Educational Conference of the Association of International Schools in Africa in Abidjan, Cote d’Ivoire (1996) and the Educational Best practices Conference of the Exam Ethics International in Accra, Ghana (2013) where I was decorated as an Exam Ethics Marshal International. I have participated in other educational conferences, seminars and workshops some of which I was the resource person. My hobbies are reading, writing and informed discussions on emerging societal issues.
